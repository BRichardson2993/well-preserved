What to do for sudden back injuries

Many Severe attacks of back pain develop from something as insignificant as bending over a bathroom sink, reaching to pick up some small item, sneezing, or reaching into low cabinets, Usually,there has been a series of small accidents leading up to this cause for the back injury. Heavy lifting falls, or severe strain is early precipitation factors in many back injuries. Usually they have occurred at some previous time and have been forgotten.

Faulty spinal dynamics are often a cause of severe back pain. Faulty spinal alignment, over a period of time will cause gradual weakening of disc fibers and other ligaments, in this weakened condition, sometimes all it take to leverage of a simple movement, such as reaching into the cupboard.

When such an incident occurs:

  1. Lie down immediately in any position that is comfortable.
  2. Have someone place 6-10 ice cubes in a plastic bag and apply this directly to the area of pain, cover with a towel, and keep the pack in place 20-30 min. this will hurt at first, but after several min. the area will be desensitized and you will feel better. Do not remove pack until 23-30 min. has elapsed
  3. Call Karissa at Dr. Tim Young Chiropractic 405-767-9750, 6020 N. Robinson Oklahoma City, OK 73118 and make arrangements to come to the office as soon as possible. In some cases, it may be suggested that you remain at home in bed for a period of time.
  4. Do not set in a recliner of any kind, as this will aggravate the condition.
  5. Maintain a positive outlook. Under Chiropractic care, back injury conditions usually respond favorably. However, it takes time, so be patient.

Are you prepared for when a tornado warning is issued?

by Anja Mottern, ARM

We are approaching the time of year when the winds pick up and people start talking about tornados. If a tornado siren goes off are you ready? Do you have a plan of action? Most people in Oklahoma are familiar with what to do in a tornado but every now and then we need a refresher. Below are some tips for when a tornado watch or warning is issued in your area:

  1. Make sure you have all of your important documents stored in an easily accessible bag. This way should a disaster happen you have your insurance policy, important numbers, pills, etc.
  2. Have a tornado emergency plan set up. In this plan you should know where the closest shelter or safe place for you to go is.
  3. Make sure that your cell phone is charged and that you have plenty of batteries, flashlights and candles.
  4. Know the difference between a watch and a warning. A watch indicates the possibility of a tornado and a warning means one has been spotted in the area.
  5. Have bottled water and nonperishable items available in case you are in a shelter for a long period of time.
  6. If you live in an apartment community make sure to check with the manager to see where the shelters are located in the building.
  7. Be aware of your neighbors and check to see if any of them need additional assistance.
  8. Make sure to listen to the radio and/or news to keep up to date. If you have a smartphone you can download a weather app to track the storms as well.

It is very important to take the weather conditions seriously and be proactive versus reactive. Your safety is the most important thing and being too cautious is better than ignoring signs!
